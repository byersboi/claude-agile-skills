# Sprint Retrospective Facilitation Formats

## Choosing a Format

| Format | Best For | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Start / Stop / Continue | Routine sprints, action focus | 45–60 min |
| Mad / Sad / Glad | Difficult sprints, team morale | 60–75 min |
| 4Ls | Milestone moments, broader reflection | 60–75 min |
| Sailboat | Identifying risks and goals | 60–90 min |
| Rose / Thorn / Bud | Balanced outlook, creative teams | 45–60 min |
| Five Whys | Root cause analysis of a specific issue | 30–45 min |
| Timeline | Complex sprints with many events | 75–90 min |
| KALM | Continuous improvement culture | 60–75 min |

---

## Start / Stop / Continue

**Structure**: Three columns — Start (new behaviours to adopt), Stop (things hindering the team), Continue (practices to keep).

**How to run**:
1. Silent writing: 5 min — everyone writes stickies for each column
2. Share and group: 15 min — read aloud, cluster duplicates
3. Dot-vote for priority: 5 min — 3 votes each
4. Action discussion: 20 min — top items become Sprint Backlog tasks with owners

**When to use**: Default format; best when team wants clear, actionable outputs.

---

## Mad / Sad / Glad

**Structure**: Three columns reflecting emotional responses to the Sprint.

**How to run**:
1. Set context: acknowledge it was a tough sprint; normalise honest sharing
2. Silent writing: 5 min — one feeling per sticky
3. Share: 15 min — read, cluster
4. Discuss root causes of Mad/Sad items: 20 min
5. Identify actions: 10 min

**When to use**: After stressful sprints; when psychological safety issues exist; when morale needs attention. Don't use if the team is not yet comfortable with vulnerability.

---

## 4Ls (Liked / Learned / Lacked / Longed For)

- **Liked**: What was enjoyable or went well
- **Learned**: New knowledge or insights gained
- **Lacked**: What was absent but needed
- **Longed For**: Aspirations, wishes, ideal-state items

**How to run**:
1. Silent writing: 8 min
2. Group by category: 15 min
3. Discussion and actions from Lacked + Longed For: 25 min

**When to use**: End of release, end of project phase, or quarterly retrospective. Provides broader reflection than sprint-level formats.

---

## Sailboat (Speed Boat / Sailboat)

**Elements**:
- **Wind** (sails): What's helping us go faster?
- **Anchors**: What's slowing us down?
- **Rocks**: Upcoming risks
- **Island**: Team's goal / destination

**How to run**:
1. Draw the sailboat visual on a whiteboard or mural
2. Silent writing: 8 min
3. Place stickies on the diagram: 5 min
4. Discuss anchors and rocks; create actions: 25 min

**When to use**: When the team needs to connect immediate work to longer-term direction or identify upcoming risks.

---

## Rose / Thorn / Bud

- **Rose**: Positives, successes
- **Thorn**: Challenges, pain points
- **Bud**: Emerging ideas, potential improvements

**How to run**:
1. Silent writing: 5 min
2. Share and cluster: 15 min
3. Focus on Buds: 20 min — which ideas are worth growing into actions?

**When to use**: Teams that respond well to growth-oriented framing; when you want to surface new ideas alongside problems.

---

## Five Whys

**Purpose**: Root cause analysis for a specific recurring problem identified by the team.

**How to run**:
1. State the problem clearly on the board
2. Ask "Why did this happen?" — write the answer
3. Repeat: ask "Why?" for each answer, 5 times (or until root cause is reached)
4. Identify countermeasure for root cause
5. Assign owner and timebox

**When to use**: When a specific issue keeps recurring across sprints. Usually follows another format that surfaces the topic for deeper analysis.

---

## KALM (Keep / Add / Less / More)

- **Keep**: What to continue doing exactly as is
- **Add**: New practices to introduce
- **Less**: Things to do less of (not stop entirely)
- **More**: Things to amplify

**How to run**:
1. Silent writing: 8 min
2. Share and cluster: 15 min
3. Prioritise and action: 20 min

**When to use**: Mature teams with a continuous improvement culture; useful as an alternative when Start/Stop/Continue feels stale.

---

## Facilitation Tips

**Psychological Safety First**
- Open with a check-in question unrelated to the Sprint (e.g., "One word to describe your energy level")
- Remind the team of the retrospective prime directive: "Regardless of what we discover, we understand and truly believe that everyone did the best job they could, given what they knew at the time, their skills and abilities, the resources available, and the situation at hand." — Norm Kerth

**Silence Before Discussion**
- Always allow 5–8 minutes of silent individual writing before group discussion. This prevents anchoring bias and ensures quieter voices are heard.

**Actions Must Be SMART**
- Every retrospective should end with ≥1 specific, owned, time-bound improvement item added to the Sprint Backlog. "We'll try to communicate better" is not an action.

**Rotate Formats**
- Vary formats across sprints to prevent habituation. Track which format was used last.

**Follow Up**
- Start each retrospective by reviewing the action from the previous one. If it wasn't done, ask why — that itself is worth retrospecting on.
